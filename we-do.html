What we do

Civil Actions

We act in cases to ensure that the police, Home Office and other state authorities are held accountable for their conduct through all available avenues. These avenues include complaint schemes, judicial reviews, and civil claims for compensation, including claims arising out of the Human Rights Act and the Equality Act.

  • Learn more

Criminal Defence

We represent individuals subject to arrest and questioning by the police (including being questioned at ports/airports). We provide advice as to their rights; if charged with criminal offences we represent them in the preparation of their cases and at their trials.

  • Learn more

Immigration

We are an experienced department practising in a wide range of immigration matters. We deal with cases from the initial application stage through to the First Tier and Upper Tribunals, higher courts and European courts. We are committed to providing high quality representation and advice.

  • Learn more

Inquests

We provide advice, assistance and representation to families whose loved ones have died in controversial circumstances involving the State and Public Bodies

  • Learn more

Prison Law

We seek to assist and advise prisoners in relation to a number of areas of Prison Service and Parole Board decision making. Where possible we challenge by way of judicial review decisions or policies that do not appear to have been applied fairly or lawfully.

  • Learn more

SIAC

Since 2001 we have been asked to act on behalf of individuals subjected to legal procedures involving secret evidence heard by the courts in closed proceedings, where neither the individuals concerned nor their lawyers have disclosed to them a major percentage of the evidence. These procedures have involved and continue to involve significant legal challenges to their validity.
